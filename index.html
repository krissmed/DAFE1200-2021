Project Rebound

Reuse, Recycle, Rebound

What is the problem?

Landfills are a common way of managing waste. The problem is that landfills produce a lot of methane, which is a greenhouse gas. It is actually 82 times more potent than CO2 (KILDE). That means that 1 kilogram of methane equals 82 kilograms CO2. In the US alone greenhouse gas emissions from landfills accounted for 15% of the US’ total methane emissions. This is approximately the same as 21 million cars pollutes in one year.

What is the solution?

Our solution to the problem is the Rebound Machine©. It removes methane from landfills by using a chemical reaction between water and methance to produce methanol and oxygen as a biproduct. Methanol is a very toxic liquid, but it can be used as fuel. Today all gas used for cars contains ~3% methanol.
